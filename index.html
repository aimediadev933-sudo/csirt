The CSIRT Asi@Connect and Mini-TRANSITS project was implemented as part of APAN44 in Dalian, China, focusing on building Computer Security Incident Response Team (CSIRT) capacity in six underrepresented Asi@Connect beneficiary economies. Through dedicated coordination meetings and training workshops, the initiative successfully addressed key challenges related to trust, information sharing, and skills development across Asia Pacific NRENs.

Project Objectives

Strategic goals for regional CSIRT development

Primary Objective

To initiate and support CSIRT development across underserved Asi@Connect economies by hosting a regional forum and training session focused on trust-building, capacity development, and incident readiness.

Sub-objectives

Regional Coordination

Organize a regional CSIRT coordination meeting at APAN44 to foster collaboration and knowledge sharing.

Training Delivery

Conduct Mini-TRANSITS training to support CSIRT formation and build essential technical capabilities.

Gap Analysis

Map CSIRT maturity and perform comprehensive gap analysis across beneficiary NRENs.

Community Building

Facilitate connections with GÉANT's TF-CSIRT and Trusted Introducer communities for ongoing support.

Project Activities

Timeline of key events and workshops

August 31, 2017

CSIRT Asi@Connect Meeting

Convened 25 participants from 18 organizations across 8 countries. Participants shared national CSIRT status and discussed challenges related to trust, collaboration, and readiness. This foundational meeting established the groundwork for regional cooperation.

September 1, 2017

Mini-TRANSITS Workshop

Conducted a compressed TRANSITS I course led by APNIC, covering CSIRT operations, incident response, and policy basics. This intensive training session was attended by 40 participants from 13 economies representing 29 organizations.

Post-Event Analysis

Comprehensive Gap Analysis

Mapped the status of CSIRT functions in 6 missing territories and broader regional partners. This analysis identified the critical need for stronger collaboration mechanisms and more accessible, affordable training programs across the region.

Ongoing

Feedback Collection & Reporting

Collected comprehensive feedback from 24 respondents across participating organizations. The overwhelming positive response, with over 90% finding the workshop "very useful", validated the approach and highlighted strong demand for continued training initiatives.

Challenges Identified

🔍 CSIRT Maturity Gap

Most teams did not meet criteria to qualify for Trusted Introducer "Listed" status, highlighting the need for continued development.

✈️ Travel Restrictions

Bhutan was unable to attend due to visa and travel documentation issues, limiting full regional participation.

🏆 Peer Recognition

Absence of accredited CSIRT peers hindered formal endorsement and listing opportunities for emerging teams.

Impact & Future Directions

Long-term vision and sustainable development goals

Future Initiatives

📚 Extended Training Programs

Pakistan and Bhutan have requested full TRANSITS I courses for national deployment, indicating strong momentum for continued capacity building.

🌐 Community Formalization

Working with APAN Task Force to embed CSIRT activities permanently within the Asia-Pacific NREN ecosystem for sustained impact.

🔄 Recurring Development

Establishing a long-term program for hands-on security training, trust-building, and continuous knowledge sharing across the region.
